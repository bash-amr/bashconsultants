What is BASH Consulting?

Plain-language definitions of the terms and services we work with. Getting these foundations right is essential, because the wrong architecture can significantly raise cost and slow a small business down.

BASH
BASH refers to Bourne Again Solutions — a Denver-based IT consulting practice that helps small and medium-sized businesses adopt enterprise-grade technology cost-effectively.
Cloud Architecture
Cloud architecture refers to the design and implementation of computing infrastructure on shared, on-demand data centers (AWS, GCP, Azure) instead of buying and maintaining your own hardware — giving small businesses enterprise capacity with no large up-front capital investment.
IaaS & SaaS
Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) refers to renting the underlying servers, storage, and networking on demand. Software as a Service (SaaS) means that ready-to-use applications are delivered over the internet. We help businesses move from owned hardware to both models.
ERP
An Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system is defined as the central software that runs a company's core operations — finance, inventory, manufacturing, and supply chain — in one integrated platform. We implement and support systems such as QAD, Infor, and Oracle.
AIS
An Accounting Information System (AIS) refers to the financial backbone that records, processes, and reports a business's transactions. We design, integrate, and support these systems — from QuickBooks to enterprise data warehouses and Excel financial models.

How does it work?

A straightforward, four-step engagement designed to fit how small businesses operate.

  1. Step 1 — Discovery consultation. We start with a free conversation to understand your business, current systems, and goals — no jargon, no obligation.
  2. Step 2 — Assessment & architecture. We map your existing tools and design a cost-effective solution, whether that's a cloud migration, an ERP rollout, or an upgraded accounting system.
  3. Step 3 — Implementation & integration. We build, configure, and integrate the solution — connecting cloud infrastructure, applications, and accounting systems so your data flows in one place.
  4. Step 4 — Training & support. We train your team and provide ongoing support, transferring the knowledge so your people can run and grow the system themselves.

Before your first call, it helps to have a few things ready:

  1. A short description of your current systems — cloud, ERP, accounting, or spreadsheets.
  2. The one or two problems that matter most to you right now.
  3. Any compliance or reporting requirements you must meet.
  4. A rough sense of timeline and who on your team will be involved.

Every engagement follows the same three principles — preparing well up front significantly improves the outcome:

  1. Empower — we give your team the right tools for the job.
  2. Educate — we transfer the knowledge and skills to use them.
  3. Engage — we stay involved so the solution actually sticks.
